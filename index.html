Rise of hate crimes againsts homosexual male and transgender people in New York in 2021

Text and photo by: Juan de Dios SÃ¡nchez Jurado

According to the data reported by the NYPD on hate crimes 2021 is showing a notable rise in the cases of violence against the LGBTQ+ community, especially trans women and homosexual male.

Between January and September this year, hate crimes against transgender people has reached 13, which means that with still three months to end the year, 2021 has reached the total of the whole 2019 and three times the total of 2020.

One of those hate crimes occured on June 22, as reported by abc7ny.com, at the Broadway and Fulton Street subway station in Lower Manhattan. A black transgender woman was harassed and stabbed by a man with a screwdriver who screamed at her transphobic remarks. She was left wounded from head to toe before the suspect got back on the A train and fled.

The number of hate crimes againts homosexual male was the second cause of hate crimes in New York in 2019 with 51 cases and the third in 2020 with 30 cases. In 2021 so far, violence against homosexual male is the third motivation for hate crimes. This year the second spot is for hate crimes against the Asian community, which from a total of 1 case in 2019 and 33 cases in 2020, rose to 130 in 2021 so far.

As reported by nbcnewyork.com Last September 4th, two gay men were attacked outside a Bushwick bodega. The first, a 36-year-old man, was punched in the face and beaten with a glass liquor bottle. The attacker then stabbed him repeatedly in the head and body. The victim suffered multiple stab wounds and a collapsed left lung. The second victim, 29, was chased down and stabbed several times while the two attackers were yelling homophobic slurs.

The percentage of hate crimes against homosexual male in New York also rised in 2021, representing the 13,5% of the total hate crimes in 2021 so far, higher than the 10,4% in 2020 and 11,4% in 2019.

This concerning data of violence against transgender people and homosexual male in New York coincides with the general alarming panorama of risk and insecurity for the LGBTQ+ community in the United States. This year was already declared the most deadly on record for trans and gender non-conforming people. According to the Human Rights Campaing, at least 48 transgender or gender non-conforming people have been fatally shot or killed by other violent means, a record that surpased the already fatal record of 44 in 2020.

The alarming growing numbers of hate crimes against trans people and homosexual male in New York adds to the already on going struggles that the LGBTQ+ community endures in the city, like the lack of housing, 30% of homeless youth identifies as gay or transgender. In regard health coverage access, according to a 2015 survey by the National Center for Transgender Equality, in the New York state, trans people are more likely to earn less and be uninsured, a problematic that applies aswell to the rest of the LGBTQ population.
